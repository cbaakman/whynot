Why Not?

Home

Why Not aims to answer a common question when working with PDB derived databanks:
Why is there no entry for ...?

To accomplish this we've looked at dependencies between PDB derived databanks (entry X can only exist if entry Y is present, or if condition Z is met) and compiled these into a databank hierarchy.

By then indexing all entries for each databank, and by comparing these with entries found higher up the hierarchy, we can find out which entries are missing. These lists can then be used to try to recreate any of the missing databank entries.

Should it be determined that for any reason a given entry can not be created, the respective databank maintainer is given the option to either manually, or automatically (based on a script or error log) annotate in Why Not the reason why a given entry can not be created.

These annotations are presented here as a convenient way to access this information.

So how do I use it?

To find out why a specific entry does not exist enter the PDBID into the search field on the right.

Or just click on any of the links on the right to drill down into collections of databank entries.
